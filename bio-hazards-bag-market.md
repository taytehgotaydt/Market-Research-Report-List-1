<p><p>The biohazard bags market comprises several types based on material characteristics. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) bags are known for their strength and resistance to chemicals, making them suitable for sharp waste. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) bags offer flexibility and are typically used for general medical waste. LLDPE (Linear Low-Density Polyethylene) provides enhanced durability and puncture resistance. PP (Polypropylene) bags are often utilized for their heat resistance. Others may include biodegradable options or laminated materials, catering to diverse waste management needs.</p></p>
